Estrategias de aprendizaje — Do's & Don'ts

✅ Do's

Study every day. Watch movies. Listen to songs. Read comic books. Play language games. Be curious. Be patient. Have fun!

❌ Don'ts

Don't be shy. Don't give up. Don't be lazy. Don't get distracted.

Simple Present — do / does
Do you speak [language]?
Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Does she / he speak [language]?
Yes, she does. / No, he doesn't.
I speak [language] and a little [language].
She speaks French and German.
Clave: -s en tercera persona: she speaks. Do → I/You/We/They. Does → She/He/It.
Modal Can — habilidad
Can you speak / read / write in [language]?
Yes, I can. / No, I can't.
I can only speak English.
She can sing French songs.
He can't read in Russian.
Clave: can nunca lleva -s con she/he/it. Siempre va seguido de verbo base.
do vs. can — contraste esencial
Simple Present (hábito / identidad)Modal Can (habilidad / capacidad)
Do you speak Spanish? → ¿Hablas español?Can you speak Spanish? → ¿Puedes hablar español?
She speaks French at home.She can sing French songs.
I don't speak Chinese.I can't read in Russian.
Does she speak other languages?Can she write in Spanish?
Imperativo + Let's
Study every day. / Have fun! / Be patient.
Don't be shy. / Don't give up.
Let's learn together.
Let's start today! / Let's try!
Let's = propuesta colaborativa. Muy útil en CLT. El imperativo negativo aparece en el brochure de estrategias (p.72).
Want to — deseos
I want to learn [language].
What language do you want to learn?
She wants to learn Portuguese.
Oral: want to → /ˈwɒnə/ (wanna) en inglés hablado. Presentar ambas formas.

Expresiones de los diálogos — discurso social (alta frecuencia)
Of course! Really? Me too! Great idea! Nice! Wow! That's amazing! That's unusual! That sounds cool! Same thing, different word! Don't worry! Let's try! It's not difficult! Languages are fun! Around the world Let's learn together! What about you?
Sugerencia: Pegar estas expresiones en tarjetas en la pizarra. Desafiar a los alumnos a usarlas durante cada conversación de la semana — son el "pegamento" del discurso oral.
Palabras con sonido /iː/ — de la unidad
Japanese cheese speak three beach sea Portuguese tea easy see eagle green tree cream please
Palabras trampa — NO tienen sonido /iː/
hello car ball match French
Actividad speed game (p.67): equipos compiten repitiendo las palabras /iː/ lo más rápido posible sin errores.
Tongue twisters — p.67
"Tea with cream for me, please."
"It's easy to see the eagle on the green tree."
Subrayar en rojo las letras con sonido /iː/ antes de leer. Pedir que las digan lento → normal → rápido.

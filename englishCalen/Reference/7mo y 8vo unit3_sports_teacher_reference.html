Lesson 1 · Time Out! · Vocabulario central

Función comunicativa: Clasificar y nombrar deportes y actividades de tiempo libre · Expresar opiniones sobre actividades · Leer un artículo de noticias deportivas · Identificar elementos de una noticia · Describir acciones simultáneas en el pasado (Past Continuous) · OA: Escuchar, leer e interactuar oralmente sobre deportes y ocio.

Nota léxica: Usar play con deportes de equipo/pelota, go con actividades -ing, do con artes marciales/individuales. Enseñar estos patrones como chunks desde el inicio.

Key Phrases — Frases clave · Lesson 1

  • What's your favorite sport?
    ¿Cuál es tu deporte favorito?
  • My favorite sport is handball because I like team sports.
    Mi deporte favorito es el handball porque me gustan los deportes en equipo.
  • Did you practice it last week?
    ¿Lo practicaste la semana pasada?
  • No, I didn't play handball last week.
    No, no jugué handball la semana pasada.
  • Which free-time activities do you enjoy?
    ¿Qué actividades de tiempo libre disfrutas?
  • I like playing cards.
    Me gusta jugar cartas.
  • What do you think about playing video games?
    ¿Qué opinas sobre jugar videojuegos?
  • In my opinion, playing video games is fun.
    En mi opinión, jugar videojuegos es divertido.
  • I think we should have basketball at school, too.
    Creo que también deberíamos tener basketball en la escuela.
  • Playing basketball is a sport. Dancing is a free-time activity.
    Jugar basketball es un deporte. Bailar es una actividad de tiempo libre.
  • While soccer player Mbappé was defending, Messi was playing…
    Mientras Mbappé defendía, Messi estaba jugando…
  • It was the greatest Soccer World Cup final of all time.
    Fue la mejor final de la Copa del Mundo de todos los tiempos.

First Next Then After that As soon as Finally While As the match was coming to an end In the end Both… and…

Key Phrases — Frases clave · Lesson 2

Grammar Structures · Unit 3: Sports & Free-Time Activities

Estructuras centrales: Past Continuous (acciones simultáneas) · Past Continuous interrumpido por Simple Past · Conectores while / when / as · Simple Past para narración · Posesivos interrogativos (Whose…?) · Presente Simple para opinión e identidad.

Past Continuous — Acciones simultáneas (Lesson 1)

While [subject] was/were + verb-ing…, [subject] was/were + verb-ing…
While Mbappé was defending, Messi was playing his last opportunity.
As the match was coming to an end, the players were beginning to taste glory.
Exercise / watch a video → He was exercising while he was watching a video.
Uso: Dos acciones en desarrollo simultáneo en el pasado. Conector clave: while. Las dos formas verbales llevan was/were + -ing. Reforzar con imágenes (p.43 — exercise/watch a video · dance/listen to music).

Past Continuous Interrumpido — Simple Past (Lesson 2)

When [simple past], [subject] was/were + verb-ing…
[Subject] was/were + verb-ing… when [simple past].
I was running with the ball when a defender kicked my ankle.
LeBron was attending high school when he led his team to three titles.
While he was living with the Walkers, he could focus on basketball.
Uso: Una acción larga (Past Continuous) es interrumpida por una acción corta/puntual (Simple Past). Conector clave: when. El juego de mímica Charades (p.52) consolida esta estructura con humor.

Punto crítico — Contraste esencial: While vs. When

Confusión frecuente en 7° básico — trabajar con ejemplos paralelos:

While + Cont. + Cont.When + Simple Past interr.
While he was defending, she was scoring.
Dos acciones largas simultáneas		 She was running when the referee blew the whistle.
Acción larga interrumpida
While we were playing, it started to rain. I was watching TV when the lights went out.

Regla visual para los alumnos:

"While" → dos líneas paralelas (igual duración)
"When" → una línea larga cortada por un punto
Actividad sugerida: Charades (p.52) — los alumnos actúan las tarjetas y el observador forma la oración completa con while/when según lo que ve. Muy efectivo como actividad de cierre.

Simple Past — Narración de eventos (ambas lecciones)

Argentina scored a goal in the 109th minute.
France missed two penalties and that made Argentina win.
Federer won his first grand slam title at Wimbledon in 2003.
LeBron was born in Akron, Ohio, USA.
Did you practice it last week? → No, I didn't play handball last week.
Uso: Narración de eventos terminados en el pasado. Muy presente en el artículo del Mundial (p.42), la audición sobre LeBron (p.48) y la escritura de la biografía (p.51).

Possessive — Whose…? (Let's Get Started)

Whose [object] is this? → It is [person]'s.
Whose car is this? → It is Lewis Hamilton's.
Whose boxing gloves are these? → They are [name]'s.
Nota: La actividad de emparejamiento (p.40) deportistas/objetos es un excelente activador de vocabulario y el posesivo Whose se presenta en contexto real y motivador.

Key Sentence Structures — Estructuras de alta rentabilidad

EstructuraEjemploFunción comunicativa
While + was/were -ing…, + was/were -ing… While Mbappé was defending, Messi was playing. Describir dos acciones pasadas simultáneas
[Subject] was -ing… when + Simple Past. I was running when a defender kicked my ankle. Narrar una acción interrumpida
Whose [object] is this? → It is [name]'s. Whose racket is this? It is Serena's. Identificar pertenencia
[Name] is a famous [sport] player. She/He was born on [date] in [place]. She/He began [sport] at the age of [n]. Saina Nehwal is a famous Indian badminton singles player. She was born on March 17, 1990 in Haryana. Escribir/contar una biografía deportiva
In my opinion, [activity] is [adjective]. In my opinion, playing video games is fun. Expresar opinión sobre deportes/actividades
[Name] had [past event]. Now, [name] [present situation]. Francisca Mardones had an accident. Now she plays paralympic sports. Contrastar pasado y presente (tabla p.40)
💡 Sugerencia docente – Grammar (Scaffolding + CLT): Presenta las estructuras gramaticales siempre en contexto narrativo real — no como reglas aisladas. Usa el artículo del Mundial (p.42) para el Past Continuous simultáneo y la anécdota del partido (p.49) para el interrumpido. La actividad Charades (p.52) es el cierre perfecto en TBL: los alumnos producen las oraciones con while/when de forma espontánea y lúdica. Para la tabla pasado/presente (p.40), comparar con los deportistas chilenos e internacionales motiva la producción oral.

Chunks y expresiones formulaicas — Lexical Approach · Unit 3

Principio: El lenguaje se aprende mejor como bloques de significado (chunks) que como palabras aisladas. Enseña estas frases como unidades completas — los alumnos las almacenan y recuperan de forma holística, acelerando la fluidez oral en contextos deportivos y narrativos.

Chunks de opinión sobre deportes

Los más frecuentes y productivos de la unidad — enseñar desde el día 1.

Opinión positiva
In my opinion, [activity] is [adjective].
Preferencia
My favorite sport is [sport] because I like [reason].
Propuesta
I think we should have [sport] at school.
Pregunta
What do you think about [activity]?
Pregunta de experiencia
Have you played [sport]? When?
Clasificación
Playing [sport] is a sport. [Activity] is a free-time activity.

Chunks narrativos — Past Continuous

Acción simultánea
While [subject] was [verb]-ing, [subject] was [verb]-ing.
Acción interrumpida
I was [verb]-ing when [Simple Past].
Anécdota inicio
It was last year while we were playing…
Clímax
Suddenly, [Simple Past event].
Resolución
In the end, [result].
Cierre
We won the game [score]-nil!

Chunks de biografía deportiva

Presentación
[Name] is a famous [nationality] [sport] player.
Nacimiento
He/She was born on [date] in [place].
Inicio
He/She began [sport] training at the age of [n].
Logros
He/She has won over [n] international titles.
Pasado→Presente
[Name] had [event]. Now, [name] [present situation].
Cierre
He/She didn't go/have/win… but today…

Chunks de discurso social — pragmática deportiva

Aparecen en diálogos y actividades sin glosa explícita. Alto valor pragmático.

make friends have fun have a good time want to win get angry sing songs taste glory feel extremely happy give your opinion slow but steady keep your boasting until you win felt pain straight away I couldn't continue well done! great match! bad luck!
Actividad: "Sports Chunk Wall" — pegar estas expresiones en tarjetas en el salón y desafiar a los alumnos a usarlas en cada conversación/anécdota deportiva durante la unidad.

Chunks de secuencia para narrar

Inicio
First, Argentina's captain converted a penalty.
Continuación
Next / Then, [event].
Giro dramático
After that, there was the extra time.
Clímax
As soon as Argentina scored, France scored too!
Resultado
Finally, France missed two penalties.
Moraleja/cierre
In the end, [winner] won the [cup/race/match].

Chunks de identidad deportiva

Posesión
Whose [object] is this? → It is [name]'s.
Hábito pasado
[Name] played [sport] for [team/years].
Situación presente
Now [name] has/plays/is…
Descripción
You [kick/throw/bounce] the ball with your [body part].
Conexión deporte-naturaleza
In the [Haka pei], participants slide down the hill at 80 km/h.
Impacto deportivo
Sports can improve your communication skills and relationships.
